  • Philosophy

    Legally, business enterprises are divided into discrete types, such as traditional for-profit companies (corps and LLCs), non-profit associations, and (more rarely) mutual benefit organizations (such as co-ops). I assist clients in navigating between these differing business regimes, or in combining elements of different business forms to create a legal structure that best serves the business and its stakeholders—for the long term.

    In doing so, I guide my clients in considering all relevant factors, including growth of the business enterprise, “exits” and other transitions, current and future sources of financing, and the roles of employees, service providers, and even customers and other patrons throughout the growth of the venture.
